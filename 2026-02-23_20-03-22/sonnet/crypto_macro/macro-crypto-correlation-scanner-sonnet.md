## RISK WARNINGS

### Correlation Breakdown Risk
Correlations between crypto and traditional assets can break down rapidly during extreme market events. While Bitcoin currently shows 60.5% correlation with the S&P 500, this relationship has historically been dynamic and unstable. During liquidity crises (March 2020, FTX collapse November 2022), correlations can spike to 1.0 (everything sells together) or invert unexpectedly. Do not rely on historical correlations as guaranteed future relationships.

### Crypto-Specific Volatility
Crypto assets remain significantly more volatile than traditional markets. Intraday swings of 5-10% are common even in "stable" conditions. On February 23, 2026, Bitcoin moved from $66,300 to $64,270 to $65,671 within 24 hours on tariff news. Thin liquidity amplifies macro-driven moves. Position sizes should account for this elevated volatility - what would be a 2% portfolio allocation in stocks might need to be 0.5-1% in crypto to achieve similar risk exposure.

### Regulatory and Policy Uncertainty
Crypto markets face ongoing regulatory uncertainty beyond traditional macro factors. While Trump's pro-crypto stance has provided tailwinds, policy can shift rapidly. The Kevin Warsh nomination as Fed Chair, potential tariff escalations affecting mining operations, and evolving crypto-specific regulations (stablecoin legislation, exchange oversight) create idiosyncratic risks uncorrelated with CPI or FOMC decisions. Diversify across assets and maintain liquidity for sudden policy shocks.

### Leverage and Liquidation Cascades
The crypto market structure includes significant leverage via perpetual futures and lending protocols. During high volatility (like the February 23 tariff-driven selloff), cascading liquidations can create feedback loops where selling triggers more selling. The "thin liquidity" mentioned in market reports means small orders can move prices significantly. Avoid excessive leverage, especially ahead of known macro events (CPI releases, FOMC meetings).

### Safe Haven Narrative Unproven
Bitcoin's "digital gold" narrative remains largely unproven in practice. As noted in the analysis, gold and Bitcoin "went in entirely different directions" in 2025, with gold at structural new highs while BTC lagged. During risk-off events, crypto is sold, not sheltered. Do not position crypto as a portfolio hedge or safe haven - it currently behaves as a high-beta risk asset with 2-3x leverage to tech stocks.

### Stablecoin and Counterparty Risk
While stablecoins (USDT, USDC) provide tactical defensive positioning within crypto, they carry counterparty risk and are not FDIC-insured bank deposits. Tether's $187B market cap and Circle's $75.7B expose holders to issuer solvency risk. Despite both companies holding significant U.S. Treasury bills, a credit event could trigger mass redemptions and de-pegging. Limit stablecoin exposure to amounts you can afford to lose and diversify across multiple issuers.

### Macro Data Surprises
Economic data can surprise in either direction, rendering positioning wrong immediately. The January 2026 PPI jumped 0.5% (massive upside surprise), catching markets off-guard. Similarly, employment, GDP, and inflation data carry significant revision risk. Initial releases can be revised substantially in subsequent months, creating whipsaw price action. Maintain flexible positioning and avoid over-committing capital based on single data points.

### Black Swan Events
Crypto markets are vulnerable to unpredictable black swan events: exchange hacks, protocol exploits, major institutional failures, geopolitical crises, or policy bans in major jurisdictions. The FTX collapse (November 2022) and Terra/Luna implosion (May 2022) demonstrate that crypto-specific crises can overwhelm macro analysis. Maintain diversification across wallets, exchanges, and asset types, and never invest more than you can afford to lose entirely.
